I love learning psychology, as I think our basic understanding of how people think, feel, and behave is a key. I aim to design user-centered products and experiences which solve both user and business problems. My process is hands-on, collaborative, and iterative.

I attended dozens of classes to study photography and art, which helped me develop visual storytelling and creative thinking.

I transitioned into UX design because I had become frustrated by how people were often prisoners to poorly designed products that made their lives more difficult.
My goal is to design products and experiences that are meaningful and impactful, and I’m especially interested in utilizing new technologies to improve people’s lives in innovative ways.
